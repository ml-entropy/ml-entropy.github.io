Why this track matters

What is an autoencoder?

An autoencoder is a neural network trained to copy its input to its output through a constrained bottleneck. That bottleneck forces the model to discover a useful code: a compressed representation that keeps the essential structure of the data while discarding redundancy and noise. The deterministic version teaches the geometry of representation learning; the probabilistic version opens the door to generative modeling.

Once the bottleneck becomes stochastic, the story stops being just about reconstruction. It becomes about inference, priors, and trade-offs between fidelity and compression. That is why VAEs sit at the intersection of deep learning, Bayesian statistics, and information theory, and why this track builds them from the ground up instead of treating them as an isolated trick.

Study modes

Choose the route that matches how you learn

The track is linear by design, but it also supports selective reading if you want intuition, implementation skill, or a theory-first reference.

Sequential path

Read 00–02 in order

Those chapters establish deterministic autoencoders, then the variational machinery, then the VAE itself. They are the conceptual spine of the whole track.

Theory route

Use 03–04 as the information core

Rate-distortion theory and the information bottleneck explain what compression objectives are really doing beneath the engineering details.

Implementation route

Pair 00, 02, 05, and 06

That combination gives the shortest path from first principles to practical model design, architecture choice, and real applications.

Prerequisites

Come in with probability and basic deep learning

You will get the most from this track if probability distributions, KL divergence, simple neural-network training, and basic linear algebra already feel comfortable.

Reading strategy

Split intuition from implementation when needed

If your goal is intuition, read the theory tabs first and leave code for a second pass. If your goal is implementation, keep the code and exercises close to chapters 00, 02, 05, and 06.

After this track

Use latent-variable thinking in larger generative systems

After completing this sequence, you will be ready to read research on representation learning, neural compression, and modern generative modeling. You will also understand why VAEs and VQ-VAEs show up inside larger systems such as latent diffusion, where a learned autoencoder shapes the space in which generation happens.
