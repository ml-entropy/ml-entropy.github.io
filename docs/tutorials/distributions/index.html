Why This Matters for ML

Probability distributions are the language of machine learning. Loss functions encode distributional assumptions (cross-entropy assumes Bernoulli, MSE assumes Gaussian). Generative models like VAEs and diffusion models learn to transform simple distributions into complex ones. Bayesian inference provides principled uncertainty quantification.

Key insight: Every probabilistic model is defined by its choice of distribution — understanding these choices unlocks deeper intuition for why models work and when they fail.
