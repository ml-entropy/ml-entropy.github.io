Why This Matters for ML

Combinatorics is the hidden engine behind probability, information theory, and algorithm design. Every time you compute entropy, evaluate a binomial likelihood, or search over discrete structures, you're doing combinatorics.

Key connections: Binomial coefficients underpin probability distributions, counting arguments drive coding theory and compression bounds, and combinatorial optimization is at the heart of neural architecture search and feature selection.
