Why This Matters

Computational biology sits at the intersection of mathematics, computer science, and life sciences. Constraint-based metabolic modeling has become a cornerstone of systems biology, enabling researchers to predict cellular behavior without detailed kinetic parameters.

These methods drive real-world impact across multiple domains:

  • Systems biology: Understanding how thousands of metabolic reactions interact to sustain life
  • Drug discovery: Identifying essential metabolic reactions as potential drug targets
  • Personalized medicine: Building patient-specific metabolic models from omics data
  • Optimization meets ML: Graph neural networks learning to solve linear programs open new frontiers in combining classical optimization with modern deep learning
