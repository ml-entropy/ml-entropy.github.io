Diffusion Models

A dedicated seven-part track on diffusion in neural networks, built from first principles. It begins with controlled corruption, passes through reverse denoising and score theory, and ends with modern fast sampling, conditioning, and architecture design.

What is Diffusion?

Diffusion models are a family of generative models that learn to create data (images, audio, video) by learning to reverse a gradual noising process. The core idea: take a clean image, slowly add random noise until it becomes pure static, then train a neural network to undo each tiny noise step. Once trained, you can start from pure noise and iteratively denoise to generate new images that look like the training data. This simple recipe — corrupt then learn to uncorrupt — turns out to be remarkably powerful, underlying systems like DALL·E, Stable Diffusion, Midjourney, and Sora.

How to use this track

Read chapters 00–02 in order if you want the mathematical story to build naturally. Those chapters establish the forward corruption process, the reverse denoising objective, and the score-based interpretation that the rest of the track depends on.

Chapters 03–06 are the advanced toolkit. They assume the core ideas from the first three chapters, but they can also be read as focused references on sampling, continuous-time formulations, guidance, and architecture design.

Prerequisites and reading strategies

You will get the most from this track if Gaussian distributions, basic neural-network training, and simple linear algebra already feel comfortable. If any of that is rusty, the Probability, Machine Learning, and Linear Algebra sections of this site make the best warm-up before diving deep into diffusion.

If your goal is intuition, read the theory tab of each chapter first and leave code and exercises for a second pass. If your goal is implementation, pair chapters 01, 03, 05, and 06: together they explain how a practical text-to-image diffusion system is trained, sampled quickly, guided by conditions, and assembled architecturally.

After this track

After completing this track, you'll have the theoretical and practical foundation to read cutting-edge diffusion papers, implement custom diffusion pipelines, and understand systems like Stable Diffusion and DALL·E from first principles. For related topics, explore the ML track's coverage of VAEs, normalizing flows, and energy-based models.
