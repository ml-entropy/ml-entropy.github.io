08. Positive Definite Matrices

Positive definite matrices are the "good" matrices in optimization. They guarantee convexity, unique minima, and well-behaved algorithms. Essential for understanding loss functions.

Definition

A symmetric matrix $A$ is positive definite if for all nonzero vectors $\mathbf{x}$:

Positive Definiteness

$$\mathbf{x}^T A \mathbf{x} > 0 \quad \text{for all } \mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{0}$$ If $\mathbf{x}^T A \mathbf{x} \geq 0$, the matrix is positive semi-definite.

The expression $\mathbf{x}^T A \mathbf{x}$ is called a quadratic form. Positive definite means the quadratic form is always positive (except at the origin).

Tests for Positive Definiteness

Equivalent Conditions
1
All eigenvalues are positive: $\lambda_i > 0$
2
All pivots are positive (in Gaussian elimination)
3
All leading principal minors are positive (Sylvester's criterion)
4
$A = R^T R$ for some matrix $R$ with independent columns (Cholesky decomposition)

Example: 2×2 Case

$$A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ b & c \end{pmatrix}$$

$A$ is positive definite if and only if: $a > 0$ and $ac - b^2 > 0$.

Geometric Interpretation

The quadratic form $f(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{x}^T A \mathbf{x}$ defines a paraboloid:

  • Positive definite: Bowl opening upward (has minimum at origin)
  • Negative definite: Bowl opening downward (has maximum)
  • Indefinite: Saddle shape (no extremum)
Connection to Convexity

A function is convex if and only if its Hessian matrix is positive semi-definite everywhere. This is why positive definiteness is central to optimization.

Cholesky Decomposition

Every positive definite matrix has a unique factorization:

$$A = LL^T$$

where $L$ is lower triangular with positive diagonal entries. This is the "square root" of $A$.

Computational Advantage

Cholesky is about twice as fast as LU decomposition and is numerically stable. It's the preferred method for solving $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$ when $A$ is positive definite (common in ML for covariance matrices).

Applications in ML

Covariance Matrices

Covariance matrices are always positive semi-definite. The variance in any direction is $\mathbf{u}^T \Sigma \mathbf{u} \geq 0$.

Hessian in Optimization

The Hessian of the loss function being positive definite at a critical point confirms it's a local minimum. Newton's method requires inverting the Hessian.

Kernel Matrices

Kernel matrices (Gram matrices) in SVMs must be positive semi-definite. This ensures the optimization problem is convex.

Regularization

Adding $\lambda I$ (ridge regularization) to $A^TA$ makes it positive definite, ensuring the system is well-conditioned and invertible.

$$(A^TA + \lambda I)\mathbf{x} = A^T\mathbf{b}$$
