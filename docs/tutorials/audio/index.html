Audio & Digital Sound Processing

From the physics of sound waves to digital signal processing, spectral analysis, psychoacoustics, and synthesis. Master the science of sound with deep intuition, mathematical rigor, and 300 hands-on problems.

Foundations

01

Sound Waves & Acoustics

What is sound? Frequency, amplitude, wavelength, the wave equation, superposition, standing waves, decibels, and the Doppler effect.
02

Digital Audio Fundamentals

Sampling, quantization, bit depth, ADC/DAC, PCM encoding, dynamic range, SNR, and dithering — converting analog sound to numbers.
03

Nyquist-Shannon Sampling Theorem

The fundamental limit: $f_s > 2f_{\max}$. Aliasing, anti-aliasing filters, sinc reconstruction, and oversampling.

Spectral Analysis

04

Fourier Transform & FFT

Decomposing sound into frequencies. Fourier series, DFT, the Cooley-Tukey FFT algorithm, convolution theorem, and practical applications.
05

Spectrograms & Time-Frequency Analysis

STFT, window functions, the uncertainty principle, spectrograms, mel spectrograms, and constant-Q transform for audio ML.

Processing & Effects

06

Digital Filters

FIR and IIR filters, transfer functions, z-transform, poles and zeros, biquad filters, and filter design for audio.
07

Audio Effects & Processing

Delay, chorus, flanger, reverb (Schroeder & convolution), dynamic compression, equalization, distortion, and pitch shifting.

Perception & Coding

08

Psychoacoustics & Human Hearing

The human ear, loudness perception, equal-loudness contours, frequency and temporal masking, spatial hearing, and perceptual models.
09

Audio Compression & Codecs

Lossless (FLAC) and lossy (MP3, AAC, Opus) compression. Perceptual coding, MDCT, psychoacoustic models, and quality metrics.

Generation

10

Sound Synthesis & Design

Additive, subtractive, FM, wavetable, and granular synthesis. Physical modeling (Karplus-Strong). ADSR envelopes.
