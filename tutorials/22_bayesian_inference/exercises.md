# Tutorial 22: Bayesian Inference Exercises

## Easy Problems

1. Write Bayes' rule for parameter inference.
2. Define prior, likelihood, posterior, and evidence.
3. What does the symbol $\propto$ mean in Bayesian derivations?
4. What is a posterior predictive distribution?
5. What is a credible interval?
6. Explain the difference between MAP estimation and full Bayesian inference.
7. If the prior is Beta and the likelihood is Binomial, what is the posterior family?
8. What information does a prior represent?
9. Why can Bayesian inference be updated sequentially?
10. Give one setting where prior information is especially useful.

## Middle Problems

1. Derive the posterior for a Beta-Binomial model.
2. Interpret the Beta prior parameters as pseudo-counts.
3. Explain why the evidence term can be hard to compute in high dimensions.
4. Compare credible intervals and confidence intervals in plain language.
5. Show how L2 regularization corresponds to a Gaussian prior in MAP estimation.
6. Explain why the posterior predictive distribution is usually wider than plugging in a point estimate.
7. Describe a case where the prior strongly influences the posterior.
8. Explain what posterior sensitivity analysis is and why it matters.
9. Compare Bayesian updating with frequentist re-estimation after each data batch.
10. Give a small numerical example of prior-to-posterior updating for coin flips.

## Hard Problems

1. Derive the posterior predictive distribution for the Beta-Binomial model.
2. Show how MAP estimation emerges from posterior optimization.
3. Explain carefully why a posterior distribution can still be misleading under model misspecification.
4. Construct an example where two reasonable priors lead to materially different conclusions.
5. Discuss the role of exchangeability in Bayesian modeling.
6. Derive the Gaussian-Gaussian conjugate posterior for an unknown mean with known variance.
7. Explain how hierarchical priors allow partial pooling across groups.
8. Write a short essay comparing Bayesian coherence with frequentist operating guarantees.
9. Describe why improper priors can create technical difficulties for model comparison.
10. Explain why exact Bayesian inference is often impossible in modern latent-variable models.
