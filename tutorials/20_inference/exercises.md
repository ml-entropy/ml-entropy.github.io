# Tutorial 20: Inference Exercises

## Easy Problems

1. Define inference in one or two sentences.
2. Give one example of an unknown quantity that inference might target.
3. Explain the difference between observed data and latent variables.
4. State one difference between inference and prediction.
5. What is an estimand?
6. What is an estimator?
7. Write a simple likelihood model $p(x \mid \theta)$ for coin flips.
8. Give one example of an assumption commonly used in inference.
9. Why does uncertainty matter in inferential conclusions?
10. Explain the phrase "reasoning backward from data."

## Middle Problems

1. Distinguish clearly between parameter inference and latent-variable inference.
2. Explain the difference between identifiability and computability with an example.
3. Describe a simple generative story for heights measured with Gaussian noise.
4. Give an example where prediction can be good even if causal inference is poor.
5. Explain why two different models might fit the same data equally well.
6. Compare the inferential targets in regression, clustering, and causal estimation.
7. Describe what repeated sampling means in classical statistics.
8. Explain how model misspecification can bias inference even with a large sample.
9. Give a real-world example where hidden confounding breaks naive inference.
10. Suppose a parameter is identifiable but expensive to compute. What kind of methods might help?

## Hard Problems

1. Formulate a general inferential pipeline for a latent-variable model from assumptions to diagnostics.
2. Construct an example where a quantity is computable but not identifiable.
3. Construct an example where a quantity is identifiable in theory but practically weakly identified.
4. Explain why uncertainty over parameters and uncertainty over future observations are not the same object.
5. Derive the likelihood for independent Gaussian observations with mean $\mu$ and known variance $\sigma^2$.
6. Argue carefully why inference necessarily depends on assumptions that cannot be read directly from data alone.
7. Discuss how selection bias can distort both frequentist and Bayesian inference.
8. Explain the difference between structural assumptions and computational approximations.
9. Give a precise example where a wrong independence assumption changes the inferred uncertainty but not the point estimate.
10. Write a short essay on the statement: "All inference is conditional on a model, explicit or implicit."
